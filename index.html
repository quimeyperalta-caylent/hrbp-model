The coverage model works because it's built on triage, leverage, and focus — not on three people trying to do everything. Each layer of the People function has a defined role, and HRBPs operate where the impact is highest.

New model · Capability

People Partners build business capability through people systems — shows up in the P&L

01 · Clear triage

Not every employee interaction needs an HRBP. Operational and transactional requests go to HR Generalists. HRBPs engage on complex, strategic, or high-stakes situations.

02 · Territory ownership

Each HRBP owns a defined territory — no overlap, no duplication. Leaders know exactly who to reach out to. That clarity alone removes a significant amount of coordination overhead.

03 · Leverage the system

HRBPs don't work alone. COEs own their domains — Comp, TA, L&D, Legal. HR Generalists handle the ground layer. HRBPs act as the connector, not the executor of everything.

04 · AI as a multiplier

AI supports analysis, synthesis, and pattern detection at scale — allowing HRBPs to spend more time on judgment, leadership partnership, and organizational decisions.

The goal is not to be everywhere — it's to be exactly where the business needs a strong people decision made. That's what makes 3 enough, for now.

09 / Structure

HR Director · Scope in the HRBP Model

The HRBP Manager leads the function with autonomy. The HR Director is a thinking partner — the person the HRBP Manager aligns with before key moments.

Shaping direction together

The HR Director and HRBP Manager define the boundaries and evolution of the model together. The HRBP Manager brings proposals and the ground-level view — the Director brings broader context and makes the final call when needed.

Alignment before key conversations

Before the HRBP Manager presents to the CPO or lands a significant decision, there's an alignment conversation with the HR Director. The HRBP Manager leads — the Director helps sharpen it beforehand.

Input on meaningful calls

Complex situations, precedent-setting decisions, or model changes include the HR Director's perspective before a path is chosen. Not a checkpoint — a genuine input into the outcome.

Final accountability

The HRBP Manager owns the day-to-day operation of the model. The HR Director holds final accountability for the People function as a whole.

Who to go to

Go to the HR Director when

A decision shapes the structure, scope, or direction of the People function — bring both the HRBP Manager and HR Director in together

The Role Definition

We defined the model.
Now we define the standard.

The consulting model — Diagnose, Advise, Act, Learn — only works if the role is built to match it. That means being clear about what capabilities this role requires, how it's positioned internally, and what it takes to operate at this level — today and as the function scales.

The shift

"HRBP as support function"

"HRBP as business capability partner"

Territory ownership

Accountable partner for a defined business territory — not reactive support

Consulting capability

Operates the Diagnose → Advise → Act → Learn model — embedded in how the role works, not layered on after the fact

Leadership enablement

Raise decision quality through coaching, calibration, and direct guidance

Org thinking

Workforce planning, talent risk, succession, spans & layers

People AI Lead

Partner with leadership on how AI changes roles, team design, and workforce capability — helping Caylent navigate that shift from the people side
