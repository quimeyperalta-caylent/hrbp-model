Why Now

Three things that aren't working

01

Incomplete coverage

Large parts of the business have no dedicated HRBP. Leaders without a partner escalate directly to the CPO — or don't escalate at all, until it's too late.

02

No shared operating standard

Without a defined model, the same situation can produce different outcomes depending on who handles it. Over time, that inconsistency erodes trust in the function.

03

Strategy and execution disconnected

No one is translating people strategy into consistent HRBP practice — or connecting what HRBPs see on the ground back up to the CPO. The signal gets lost in both directions.

For Caro & Jen

HR partnership is what makes this work

The HRBP model and the HR Generalist function are stronger together. This works when both layers are clear on scope, aligned on goals, and communicating well — not when one replaces the other.

What HR Generalists handle

●First point of contact for ICs on regional and local matters
●Benefits, compliance, local policy questions, reimbursements
●High-volume transactional HR — onboarding paperwork, documentation, system updates
●Regional pulse — first to catch the signals that HRBPs need to act on

What HRBPs bring

●Strategic partnership with Directors and Managers
●Complex, sensitive, or escalated IC situations
●Performance, talent, org design decisions
●Anything that sets precedent or crosses practices

How we work together

●Escalation path: IC → HR Generalist for day-to-day → HRBP for complex or sensitive situations → HRBP Manager for anything that sets org precedent
●Regular alignment: Bi-weekly check-in between HRBP team and HR Generalist leads — shared cases, regional signals, emerging patterns
●Demand signals: HR Generalists flag recurring IC issues to HRBPs — this is how we catch systemic problems early
●Future hiring: When the team scales, HR Generalists will be key partners in candidate experience and onboarding logistics for new HRBPs
"The model does not make HR less reachable. It makes the right resource easier to find."
